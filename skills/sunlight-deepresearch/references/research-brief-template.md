# Research Brief Template

Use this template before dispatching subagents. Keep it concise; the brief should guide research, not become the report.

```md
# Research Brief

## Objective
<What the research should answer.>

## Audience
<Who will use the report.>

## Decision Context
<What decision, strategy, or understanding this research should support.>

## Scope
- Entities, competitors, markets, claims, or topics:
- Timeframe and recency needs:
- Geography or segment:
- Explicit exclusions:

## Source Requirements
- Required source types:
- Preferred primary sources:
- Sources to avoid:
- Citation expectations:

## Output Format
<Report, memo, comparison table, recommendation, briefing, evidence map, etc.>

## Success Criteria
- <What must be true for the research to be useful.>
- <What questions must be answered.>

## Clarification Needed
<One concise question if the brief is not clear enough; otherwise write "none".>
```

## Clarification Rule
Ask a clarifying question only when missing information would prevent useful decomposition. Do not ask for every preference up front; make reasonable assumptions and state them when the research can proceed.
