# Research Brief

## Objective
Answer how Figma should position and operate in the AI age, with emphasis on whether it should compete as a raw UI generator or as a product-context layer for AI-assisted product teams.

## Audience
Product, strategy, and design-technology readers evaluating Figma's AI strategy.

## Decision Context
The report should support a strategic point of view about Figma's defensible role as AI makes interface generation cheaper.

## Scope
- Entities, competitors, markets, claims, or topics: Figma, Figma Make, Sites, Buzz, Draw, Dev Mode, MCP, Code Connect, AI app builders, design-to-code, design systems, governance, and AI-credit monetization.
- Timeframe and recency needs: 2025-2026 product and business evidence, with current 2026 coverage where available.
- Geography or segment: Global software/product teams, with enterprise and collaborative product-development emphasis.
- Explicit exclusions: No valuation model, stock recommendation, or exhaustive competitor teardown.

## Source Requirements
- Required source types: official filings, reputable product/news coverage, practical workflow evidence, and risk/security evidence.
- Preferred primary sources: SEC filings and official product documentation or announcements where available.
- Sources to avoid: unsourced opinion posts, SEO summaries, and claims that cannot be tied to a URL.
- Citation expectations: Every key finding and factual sentence in the final report needs an inline source ID or direct markdown link.

## Output Format
Sourced strategy memo with evidence files, subagent summaries, evaluator audits, and a final report.

## Success Criteria
- The report explains the strategic answer and why it follows from evidence.
- The report links key factual claims to registered sources.
- The report passes the citation-coverage evaluator.
