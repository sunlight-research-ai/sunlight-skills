# Research Plan

## Classification
mixed

## User Intent
Run a real Figma deep research report, using the updated sunlight-deepresearch workflow, then evaluate whether the output meets the goal of source-linked, verifiable research.

## Constraints
- Recency: Prioritize 2025-2026 sources because Figma's AI, MCP, product, and earnings context is current.
- Required sources: Primary or authoritative business/product sources where possible, plus secondary evidence for competitive, workflow, and risk claims.
- Excluded sources: Unsourced summaries and snippet-only claims unless later verified by opened source pages.
- Output format: File-backed deep research run with final report and evaluator artifacts.
- Named entities: Figma, Figma Make, Sites, Buzz, Draw, Dev Mode, MCP, Code Connect, AI app builders, Canva, Adobe, Webflow, Framer, v0, Lovable, Bolt, Cursor, Claude Code, Codex-style agents.
- Geography / market: Global product-development and design-tool market.
- Evaluation dimensions: Strategic defensibility, product evidence, monetization evidence, AI workflow evidence, governance and risk evidence, source coverage.
- Other constraints: Use source IDs consistently and deduplicate URLs in the registry.

## Research Tracks

### Track 1: Platform Positioning and Product Surface
- Objective: Determine whether Figma's product expansion supports a platform/control-plane thesis.
- Dimension: Product strategy.
- Named entities: Figma Sites, Make, Buzz, Draw, Dev Mode, Code layers, Motion, Shaders, Weave.
- Source hints: The Verge Config coverage, official Figma material, SEC filings.
- Questions to answer: What product surfaces has Figma added, and do they broaden Figma beyond static design?
- Expected output: Source-tagged findings about Figma's expanding platform footprint.

### Track 2: AI Workflow, Context, and Handoff
- Objective: Determine whether Figma's AI advantage is context-aware generation rather than generic generation.
- Dimension: AI workflow and technical handoff.
- Named entities: Figma Make, MCP, Code Connect, Claude, Cursor, Windsurf, VS Code, AI agents.
- Source hints: MCP coverage, Make GA coverage, practical MCP workflow articles.
- Questions to answer: How does Figma connect design context to AI coding and prototyping workflows?
- Expected output: Source-tagged findings about context, code visibility, design references, and limitations.

### Track 3: Business Model, Governance, and Risk
- Objective: Determine whether Figma has economic and governance reasons to own AI product context.
- Dimension: Monetization, enterprise governance, and risk.
- Named entities: AI credits, full seats, paid customers, net dollar retention, MCP security, design-to-code quality.
- Source hints: SEC filings, earnings coverage, AI-credit coverage, security coverage.
- Questions to answer: What business signals and risks shape Figma's AI strategy?
- Expected output: Source-tagged findings about revenue, AI monetization, governance needs, and risks.

## Optional Context Track
Competitive framing is handled inside the three tracks rather than as a separate broad competitor inventory.
